# Replications-Extensions-American-Economic-Association
A backlog of replication exercises that tests, verifies and reports on robustness / sensitivity of published AEA published research. 

The backlog of replications represents STATA DO files including the datasets needed to replicate the initial findings of American Economic Journal published research. The process is expanded however through providing additional review of robustness / sensitivity and overall implications of findings for which can be found in the PDF documents that analyse the findings through both, quantitative, qualitative and historical assumptions and implications. 

Please feel free to utilise the DO files to reproduce and expand the research yourself. I have provided 2 key examples of how such a replication and extension can take form. 

Replication and Extension Examples: 
(1) Nunn, N. (2008). The long-term effects of Africa's slave trades. The Quarterly journal of economics, 123(1), 139-176.

(2) Toney, J., & Robertson, C. L. (2021, May). Intergenerational economic mobility and the racial wealth gap. In AEA Papers and Proceedings (Vol. 111, pp. 206-210). 2014 Broadway, Suite 305, Nashville, TN 37203: American Economic Association.
